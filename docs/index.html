So, just who is this Perris Davis?

A short biography, told slant.

I am a lifelong learner, a social advocate, and an iOS developer. Before I wrote software for iPhones, I repaired them. Before that, I lobbied Congress on behalf of students and veterans, ran a small consulting business out of Ellensburg, and graduated from Central Washington University with a degree in political science & public policy.

I taught myself object-oriented programming through Swift, dependency management through Cocoapods, and version control through Git. The first app I ever shipped* was a small puzzle game called bumplock; I have been quietly shipping ever since.

These days I'm interested in local-first software, beautiful typography on small screens, the unreasonable effectiveness of a well-written README, and how policy decisions become product decisions three layers down.

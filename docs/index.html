So, just who is this Perris Davis?

A short biography, told slant.

I am, in roughly the order it happened, a public-policy student, a small-business owner, a lobbyist on behalf of students and veterans, an iOS device repair tech, and — for the last decade — a working software engineer. The throughline is plainer than the résumé suggests: I like figuring out how complicated systems actually behave, and then explaining or rebuilding them so they behave better.

iOS is my primary craft. I taught myself object-oriented programming through Swift, shipped my first app* — a small puzzle game called bumplock — in 2016, and have been quietly shipping ever since. Alongside the code I keep up a small writing practice, the occasional consulting engagement, and a stubborn interest in how policy decisions become product decisions three layers down.

These days I'm most interested in local-first software, beautiful typography on small screens, the unreasonable effectiveness of a well-written README, and software that respects the people on the other side of the glass.
